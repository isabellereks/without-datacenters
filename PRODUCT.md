# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Curious readers who have heard arguments about AI, data centers, climate, water, jobs, privacy, or internet dependency, but have not yet connected those debates to an ordinary day. They are likely skeptical of hype and allergic to corporate gloss.

## Product Purpose

This site is an illustrative storybook about a day without data centers. It should help people feel the tradeoff: some harmful systems go quiet, but so do many invisible services people rely on for care, work, school, money, logistics, accessibility, memory, and connection.

Success means a reader finishes with a more nuanced question: not "data centers good or bad," but "what should exist, who should benefit, who should pay, and what should never have been built on this infrastructure?"

## Brand Personality

Human, curious, and gently unsentimental. The voice should feel like a thoughtful picture book for adults: warm enough to stay with, precise enough to be trusted, and honest about harms without becoming scolding or cold.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI illustration, beige SaaS softness, dashboard chrome, preachy explainers, crisis-poster alarmism, and Apple mimicry that copies surface styling without the human-centered pacing.

## Design Principles

Lead with a human moment before explaining the system.

Make infrastructure feel physical, local, and ordinary.

Give critics their strongest point before showing the cost of total absence.

Keep the interface quiet enough that the reader feels like they are turning pages, not operating software.

Use sources as receipts, not as academic clutter.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for readable contrast, keyboard navigation, reduced-motion support, and clear source links. The experience should remain usable on mobile, where a storybook interface can easily become cramped.
